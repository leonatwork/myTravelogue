My travelogue

This summer I had been to Kerala and Bangalore. Kerala is often referred to as God's own Country and is blessed abundantly with serene natural beauty; from calm beaches to the mighty fog-laden hills, the surreal backwaters and plethora of ecological diversities, from the guileless elephants to the towering coconut trees. Kerala has many beautifull artistic churches and temples which will surely leave anyone spellbound. With such a majestic milieu you certainly are gonna feel closer to the God's heavenly abode. Bangalore, a bustling city with a lots of high-rise buildings, techno parks and traffic, mostly constituting a hell lot of the IT bunch running the Indian Silicon valley, but still in its heart Bangalore is an old soul also known as the Garden city with many lakes and gardens and most importantly having wonderful mild climate all-round the year. This travelogue is filled with my reminiscences of this trip.

THE GOD'S OWN COUNTRY

Kerala rightly called The God's own Country is sanctified by numerous picturesque landscapes. It's certainly one of those place you got to have in your bucket list. Standing on top of these hills you will certainly want to breathe in the nature and soak in the last bit of memory with nature's brimming beauty to cherish forever.

RUBBER PLANTATIONS

Rubber plantations is a common sight in the villages in Kerala. With the tall rubber trees planted in a systemized fashion you can barely see the sunlight penetrating through its branches. Its truly a sigth to behold.

THE GARDEN CITY

Bangalore is bestowed with many alluring gardens which will surely entice anyone. These places are certainly a hotspot among fitness enthusiasts, lovebirds and those who want to take a break from the city's evergrowing hustle and bustle.

IT PARKS

The city has many IT and techno parks, housing the techno Giants of the world, with its tall skyscrapers only adding to its grandeur. With continuous growth and development the region has earned the name of "Indian Silicon Valley"
